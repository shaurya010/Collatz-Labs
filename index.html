Why We Exist

Two trends. One unavoidable question.

Post-quantum cryptography standards are here — NIST FIPS 203, 204, and 205 mean organizations now need to audit their codebases for the primitives a sufficiently large quantum computer would break: RSA, elliptic curves, classical Diffie-Hellman.

The natural way to run that audit at scale is to hand code to an automated scanner. That raises the question we were founded to answer: how does a codebase owner prove a fact about private source code to a third party, without handing over the code itself?

The assessment still has to happen somewhere with full visibility — that can't be avoided if the goal is finding what you don't already know to look for. What can be avoided is that visibility ever leaving your own boundary.

How It Works

The same three moves, across every product.

01

Discover, in your boundary

A deterministic engine runs locally, in your CI, or in a TEE — wherever your data already sits. Nothing here is designed to leave.

02

Redact and commit

Findings are trimmed to the minimum context needed, then committed into a Merkle tree so any one fact can be proven without exposing the rest.

03

Sign and share

A signed attestation — score, proofs, redacted snippets, nothing else — is the only artifact that ever reaches an auditor, customer, or regulator.
